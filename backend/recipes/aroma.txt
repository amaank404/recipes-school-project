# Description
Aroma is a classic Middle Eastern dish made with layers of crispy phyllo dough, savory meat filling, and a blend of aromatic spices. This recipe combines the flavors of lamb, onions, garlic, and herbs to create a rich and satisfying main course or appetizer.

~DESCRIPTION_SEP_IDENTIFIER~

# Ingredients
- 1 package of phyllo dough (thawed)
- 1 pound ground lamb
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
- 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 egg, beaten
- Vegetable oil for brushing phyllo
- Feta cheese (optional)

# Instructions
To make the Aroma:
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
3. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes.
4. Add the garlic, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper, and stir for 1 minute.
5. Add the ground lamb and cook, breaking up with a spoon, until browned, about 5-7 minutes.
6. Stir in the chopped parsley, mint, lemon juice, and beaten egg.
7. Unroll the phyllo dough and cut into 4 equal pieces.
8. Brush each piece of phyllo with vegetable oil and layer on a baking sheet.
9. Spoon the meat mixture onto one half of each phyllo square, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.
10. Fold the other half of the phyllo over the filling to form a triangle or square shape, pressing edges to seal.
11. Brush the tops of the pastries with additional vegetable oil and sprinkle with feta cheese (if using).
12. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown.
13. Serve warm and enjoy!

# Tips and Variations
- To add extra flavor, try adding some chopped sun-dried tomatoes or Kalamata olives to the meat mixture.
- For a vegetarian version, substitute the ground lamb with cooked spinach or mushrooms.
- Experiment with different types of cheese, such as crumbled goat cheese or shredded mozzarella.
- To freeze the Aroma, assemble the pastries without baking and store them in an airtight container for up to 2 months. Simply bake frozen pastries at 375°F (190°C) for an additional 15-20 minutes.
