Homemade Chicken Broth
~RECIPE_NAME_SEP_IDENTIFIER~

Servings: 4-6 people
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
Total Time: 2 hours

~INFO_SEP_IDENTIFIER~

# Description
A rich and flavorful homemade chicken broth made with roasted bones, vegetables, and aromatics. This broth is perfect for soups, stews, or as a base for sauces and gravies.

~DESCRIPTION_SEP_IDENTIFIER~

# Ingredients
- 2 lbs chicken bones (necks, backs, and wings)
- 2 carrots, chopped
- 2 celery stalks, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 4 sprigs fresh parsley
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 tsp black peppercorns
- 1 tsp salt
- 4 quarts water

... 
- Optional: 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar

~INGREDIENTS_SEP_IDENTIFIER~

# Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Place the chicken bones on a baking sheet and roast in the oven for 30 minutes, or until lightly browned.
3. In a large stockpot, sauté the chopped onion, carrots, and celery in a little bit of oil until tender, about 5-7 minutes.
4. Add the roasted bones, garlic, thyme, parsley, bay leaf, peppercorns, and salt to the pot. Pour in the water and bring to a boil.
5. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 1 hour 30 minutes, skimming off any impurities that rise to the surface.
6. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean pot, discarding the solids.
7. If using apple cider vinegar, add it to the broth and stir to combine.
8. Let the broth cool, then refrigerate or freeze for later use.

~INSTRUCTIONS_SEP_IDENTIFIER~

# Tips and Variations
- To make a clear broth, skim off any fat that rises to the surface after cooling.
- For a more intense flavor, use 6-8 pounds of bones and roast them for an additional 15-20 minutes.
- Experiment with different herbs and spices, such as lemongrass or star anise, to create unique flavor profiles.
- Use this broth as a base for soups like chicken noodle soup or creamy broths.
